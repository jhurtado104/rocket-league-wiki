Competitive Ranks

In addition to casual play, there is a competitive side to the game that players challenge themselves with but will focus on competitive 3v3s. The ranking system starts from Bronze and goes as high as Supersonic Legend. In each rank, there are three tiers I, II, and III. In each tier there are divisions and there are four divisions I, II, III, IV. It sounds difficult at first but once the player enters the competitive side of the game they will quickly understand it a bit better. The way you climb the ranks goes from I, being to lowest, and III, being the highest tier. It works the same in each division with I, being the lowest, and IV is the highest division. For example, a player could be in Champion I Division III. After every season, the player will be awarded exclusive rank rewards. These rewards can vary from goals explosions to boosts but are only cosmetic items and nothing that will give a player an advantage over another player. Players typically have about 3-4 months, within a season, to achieve these exclusive rewards, but once the season is over these items can't be obtained. To obtain these rewards, players have to win 10 matches in each rank from Bronze up to their rank. For example, if a player is in Grand Champion they will have to upgrade their season reward by winning 10 games to advance to Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond, Champion, and Grand Champion before the season ends. Ranking up is quite simple to the player by winning games getting you through the ranks and losing games leading you to lose the rank, but behind the scenes, the game goes by MMR (matchmaking rank) which doesn't show the player. Luckily players can use the website Rocket League tracker. Another way that players can see their MMR is by using a plug-in called Bakkes Mod which can show their MMR while in-game. It will also show the number of points the player loses from a loss and how much they gain after a win. Below you can see the ranks and MMR ranges for competitive 3v3s.
